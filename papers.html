design of efficient full adder using qca
This paper explores the design and optimization of a Quantum-Dot Cellular Automata (QCA) full adder, a critical component for QCA-based arithmetic circuits. By utilizing two 2-input XOR gates and a majority gate, the proposed single-layer coplanar design achieves reduced cell count, area, and latency. Comparative analysis highlights a 16.95% improvement in cell count and a 22.22% reduction in the cost function compared to prior designs. Simulation and power analysis validate its efficiency, low latency, and reliability. The results showcase potential applications in advanced digital systems like microprocessors and compressors.
deep learning based cooperative relay selection in virtual mimo(btp)
The project, Deep Learning Based Cooperative Relay Selection in Virtual MIMO, focuses on enhancing cooperative communication systems by leveraging deep learning. It aims to optimize relay selection in Virtual Multiple Input Multiple Output (V-MIMO) networks using prediction algorithms like ANN and SVM. The work reduces waiting time at the eNodeB, incorporates real-world datasets, and evaluates metrics such as battery power and transceiver module capability. The study also integrates security protocols to safeguard source user information during transmission.
